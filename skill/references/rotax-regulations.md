# Rotax MAX Challenge Regulations Reference

This reference covers rules and regulations for the Rotax Micro MAX and Mini MAX classes.
When answering rules questions, cite the specific section. If you're unsure about a rule,
say so and recommend the user check the official Rotax Racing website or their local
series regulations, as some details vary by country/series.

**Official source:** [rotax-racing.com/rmc-regulations](https://www.rotax-racing.com/rmc-regulations)

## 1. Class definitions

### Micro MAX
- **Age:** Driver must turn 8 during the calendar year of the event. Must not yet have
  turned 12 (so ages 8-11 eligible).
- **Engine:** Rotax 125 Micro MAX EVO — junior cylinder without exhaust valve, restricted
  exhaust socket.
- **Power:** ~7 kW / 9.5 hp at 7,000 RPM
- **Torque:** ~10.8 Nm at 6,000 RPM

### Mini MAX
- **Age:** Driver must turn 10 during the calendar year. Must not yet have turned 14
  (ages 10-13 eligible). Some series set tighter age bands — check local rules.
- **Engine:** Rotax 125 Mini MAX — slightly less restricted than Micro, more power.
- **Power:** ~13 hp at peak
- **Peak torque RPM:** ~7,500 RPM

Both classes use a centrifugal clutch with single-speed chain drive (no gearbox).
There is no manual gear shifting — the clutch engages automatically.

## 2. Engine specifications

### Common to both Micro and Mini MAX
- **Displacement:** 125cc single-cylinder, 2-stroke
- **Cooling:** Water-cooled with integrated water pump
- **Ignition:** Digital battery ignition system
- **Starting:** Electric starter (integrated)
- **Features:** Balance shaft, integrated water pump
- **Drive:** Chain-driven single speed with centrifugal clutch
- **Primary sprocket:** 14-tooth (included with engine)

### Gearbox internals (sealed, not serviceable)
- Primary shaft: 19 teeth (1st gear), 24 teeth (2nd gear)
- Idle gear: 81 teeth (1st), 77 teeth (2nd)
- Balance shaft identification code: 6237948 or 6237949

### Engine weight
- Bare engine: 11.8 kg / 26.0 lbs
- Complete power pack: 21.5 kg / 47.5 lbs

## 3. Sealed components and homologation

**Fundamental rule:** Anything not expressly permitted in the technical regulations is
**forbidden**. This is a sealed-engine formula — the engine must remain in homologated
condition.

Components that must remain sealed/unmodified:
- Cylinder and cylinder head
- Crankcase and crankshaft assembly
- Balance shaft
- Ignition system (DENSO digital)
- Exhaust system (must use homologated exhaust with any specified restrictors)
- Carburettor body (specific model mandated per class)

**You cannot:**
- Port or polish any engine component
- Modify the exhaust or add aftermarket components
- Change the ignition timing or mapping
- Use non-homologated gaskets, seals, or o-rings
- Modify the reed valve assembly
- Change the combustion chamber volume

**You can (generally):**
- Change external sprockets (rear) within allowed range
- Adjust carburettor jetting (main jet, needle position, idle screw)
- Replace wear items (piston, ring, bearings) with genuine Rotax parts
- Adjust clutch springs within homologated options
- Clean and maintain — but not modify

## 4. Carburettor

### Micro MAX
- **Model:** Dellorto PHBG (specific bore size mandated — typically 18mm)
- **Needle:** Mandated specification (D55 type, clip position is free)
- **Main jet:** Free within Dellorto range (this is a tuning variable)
- **Idle jet:** Free within Dellorto range
- **Float height:** Adjustable (16 ±0.5mm is the baseline for PHBG 18)

### Mini MAX
- Similar Dellorto PHBG carburettor, possibly different bore or needle spec
  depending on series year

### Jetting guidelines (for reference only — verify against conditions)
- UK summer ~15°C, sea level: approximately 148 main jet on Mini MAX
- Jetting varies significantly with altitude, temperature, and humidity
- Always read the spark plug and/or EGT data to confirm mixture
- Richer jetting (larger main jet number) for cold/humid conditions
- Leaner jetting (smaller main jet number) for hot/dry conditions

**Important:** Carburettor body must remain unmodified. No drilling, boring,
or external air filters unless specified in regs. Intake silencer/airbox as
homologated.

## 5. Weight minimums

### Micro MAX
- **Minimum weight:** 105 kg — complete kart + driver + all equipment worn during race
- This includes: kart chassis, engine, fuel, driver in race suit, helmet, gloves,
  boots, rib protector, and any ballast

### Mini MAX
- **Minimum weight:** 115 kg — same measurement criteria

**Weighing procedure:**
- Weighed immediately after the session/race (no adding fuel or equipment)
- All equipment the driver was wearing during the race must be worn at weighing
- Ballast must be securely bolted to the chassis (specific mounting rules apply)
- Failure to make weight = disqualification from that session/race

**Strategy note:** Being right at minimum weight is ideal. Extra weight slows the kart.
If the driver is well under the minimum, ballast placement affects handling — lower and more
central is better.

## 6. Tires

### Approved tires
Each series specifies the approved tire compound. Common options in Rotax:
- **MG** tires (various compounds: Red, White, Yellow, etc.)
- **Mojo** tires (used in many Rotax series globally)
- **LeCont** tires (some European series)

Check your specific series regulations for the mandated compound. Using non-approved
tires = disqualification.

### Tire rules
- Maximum number of tire sets per event may be limited
- Tires may be marked/barcoded for identification
- Tire warmers are typically **not permitted** in Cadet classes
- Chemical tire treatments are **strictly prohibited** and will result in
  exclusion from the event
- Tires must be commercially available Rotax-approved compounds

### Tire pressure guidelines (general starting points — NOT regulations)
- **Dry conditions:** Cold pressures typically 0.82-0.90 bar (12-13 psi),
  targeting hot pressures of 1.00-1.10 bar (14.5-16 psi)
- **Damp conditions:** Start slightly higher cold, ~0.95-1.05 bar (14-15 psi)
- **Wet:** Higher still, ~1.05-1.15 bar (15-17 psi)
- These vary by compound, track, ambient temp, and driver weight

## 7. Fuel

- **Approved fuel:** Standard pump fuel as specified by series (typically 95-98 octane
  unleaded). Some series mandate a specific fuel supplier at the event.
- **Fuel additives:** Prohibited. No octane boosters, no oil additives beyond the
  required 2-stroke oil.
- **Oil mix ratio:** As specified by Rotax (typically pre-mixed or injected per engine
  spec). Check your series for the mandated ratio.
- **Fuel testing:** Random fuel samples may be taken. Non-compliant fuel =
  disqualification.

---

## 8. Chassis

- Must use a CIK-FIA homologated chassis (or series-approved chassis)
- Minimum wheelbase and track width specifications apply
- No aerodynamic bodywork beyond the standard Cadet fairing/bodywork
- **No front wings** — Cadet karts do not have front aerodynamic elements
- Nassau panel (front fairing), side pods, and rear bumper must be fitted
- All bodywork must meet CIK-FIA crash testing standards
- Numbers must be displayed per series-specific positioning rules

---

## 9. Safety equipment

**Mandatory (driver):**
- CIK-FIA or FIA-approved helmet (current homologation)
- Balaclava (fire-resistant)
- Racing suit (CIK-FIA approved)
- Gloves (karting-specific)
- Rib protector (strongly recommended, mandatory in most series)
- Racing boots covering the ankle
- Neck brace/collar recommended for Cadet drivers

**Mandatory (kart):**
- Functional rain light (rear)
- Kill switch accessible to the driver
- Chain guard
- Rear bumper and side pods securely mounted
- Minimum 2 rear-facing reflectors (some series)

## 10. Sporting regulations

### Flag signals
| Flag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Green** | Track clear, racing underway |
| **Yellow** | Caution — danger on track, no overtaking in that zone |
| **Red** | Session stopped immediately — return to pit/grid slowly |
| **Blue** | You are about to be lapped — let the faster kart through safely |
| **Black** | Report to race control immediately (typically a penalty) |
| **Black with orange circle** | Mechanical problem — return to pits |
| **Chequered** | Session/race finished |

### Penalties
- **Ignoring blue flags:** Warning, then time penalty, then exclusion for repeat offenses
- **Dangerous driving:** Penalties from reprimand to exclusion depending on severity.
  Includes: premature direction changes, deliberate crowding, more than one defensive
  move, dangerous re-entry after leaving the track.
- **Contact causing advantage:** Time penalty or position penalty
- **Jump start:** Time penalty added to race time (typically 5 or 10 seconds)
- **Black flag:** Driver excluded from session; scored last in classification
- **Weight undercut:** Disqualification from that session

### Key driving rules for the driver
- **One defensive move only.** You can move once to defend, then you must hold your line.
  Weaving is illegal and will be penalized.
- **Give racing room.** If a kart is alongside you into a corner, you must leave space.
- **Blue flags matter.** If you see a blue flag, facilitate the pass safely. Don't
  block — it's a penalty.
- **Track limits.** All four wheels must remain within track boundaries. Consistent
  track limit violations will be penalized.

## 11. Race procedures

### Race weekend structure (typical)
1. **Free practice** — open sessions for setup and driver warm-up
2. **Qualifying practice** — timed session to set grid position (fastest lap counts)
3. **Heats** — short races to determine final grid positions (points-based or averaging)
4. **Pre-final** — sets the grid for the final
5. **Final** — the main race that determines the result

### Standing start procedure (Rotax Cadet)
1. Formation lap behind a pace car or lead kart
2. Karts take grid positions (2x2 grid formation)
3. When all karts are stationary, a green flag marshal signals ready
4. Clerk of the course raises and drops the national flag, or lights go out
5. Race begins — any movement before the signal = jump start penalty

### Parc fermé
After qualifying and finals, karts go directly to parc fermé (a secured area).
Drivers and mechanics cannot touch the karts until released. This is where tech
inspection happens. Removing equipment or adding anything = disqualification.

## 12. Tech inspection preparation

Before every race weekend, check:

- [ ] Engine seals intact and visible
- [ ] Correct carburettor fitted (matching homologation sticker)
- [ ] Spark plug is Rotax-approved type
- [ ] Exhaust restrictor in place (Micro MAX)
- [ ] All bodywork securely mounted with proper hardware
- [ ] Numbers correctly positioned and legible
- [ ] Rain light functional
- [ ] Kill switch functional
- [ ] Weight check with full equipment (target 1-2 kg over minimum for safety margin)
- [ ] Tire markings match registered set
- [ ] Fuel is approved specification
- [ ] Chain guard fitted
- [ ] No non-homologated modifications anywhere

**Pro tip:** Do a full self-tech-inspection the night before the race. Finding a problem
Saturday morning is stressful and wastes track time.
